I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in the Department of Government at Harvard University, where I am advised by [Gary King](https://gking.harvard.edu). My research examines how economic globalization shapes domestic political economies—studying how exposure to international trade and capital flows affects policy preferences, partisan alignment, and distributional conflict in advanced industrialized democracies.

My work combines large-scale observational data with causal inference methods, including difference-in-differences designs, regression discontinuity, and computational text analysis. I am particularly interested in how aggregate shocks from trade and foreign direct investment translate into divergent political responses at the individual and community level.

Before beginning the PhD, I worked as a research analyst at a policy research institution focused on international economics and labor market policy. I hold a B.A. in Economics and Political Science from the University of Chicago (2021), where I wrote my undergraduate thesis on trade adjustment assistance and its political consequences.

I am an affiliate of the [Institute for Quantitative Social Science](https://iqss.harvard.edu) (IQSS) at Harvard. My research has been supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship and the Harvard Merit Fellowship.
